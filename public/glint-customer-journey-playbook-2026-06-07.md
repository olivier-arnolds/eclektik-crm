## 1. What your data actually shows today

**The delivery model is transactional, one cycle at a time.** Across the War room projects the shape repeats: a block of **CS hours** (platform implementation + survey configuration) and a block of **PS hours** (one Insight Review), delivered around a single survey, then the relationship goes quiet until the next survey is contracted. Examples:

- **Survey + one readout** is the dominant unit: Dentsu (1 Insight Review), Draper (1 IR), Warburtons, Alex Lee, Jazz, BioMarin — all "configure → field → one presentation."
- **Hours run out mid-relationship.** Serco "CS time used up, now on T&M 8hrs"; Sage "64 hours of Kirsty's budget still to be used"; repeated notes about re-activating PS work contract-by-contract (Maire).
- **Gaps between surveys are unmanaged.** Breitling: "contact again in May to follow up" after a March contract end. Most clients have no booked touchpoint between cycles.
- **Churn signals are real.** GoDaddy "moving to Qualtrics 2nd half of 2026." That is the cost of a transactional model — when you're the survey vendor, not the embedded advisor, you're replaceable.

**Meanwhile the PS analyses are far more valuable than a one-off deck.** The People Science DB already produces, per cycle: engagement score vs benchmark and YoY, response rate, a narrative `summary`, **top-3 next-best-actions** (`top_3_nba`), an **opportunity-shift** read, **risks**, **strategy mapping**, and a four-lens diagnostic (JD-R, Kahn, Saks, STS, Palgrave). Look at the Sage GTM read: *"Customer Focus 72 (-8 BM, Very High impact) + 2,329 AI-concern comments… these are one coupled item… launch Customer Win of the Month + AI Guardrails one-pager, name a dashboard owner."* That is **boardroom-grade advisory** being delivered once, as a slide, and then left on the table.

**The gap in one sentence:** you sell the *survey and the readout*; Microsoft's own model — and the value you're already capable of — is the *continuous listen → act → re-listen loop*, and the whole "act" half of that loop is where a professional-services firm deepens the relationship and earns more.

## 2. The canonical Viva Glint customer journey (Microsoft's model)

Anchored to a survey cycle (T0 = survey opens, Tc = survey closes). This is Microsoft's prescribed flow, not ours:

| Phase | Timing | What happens (per MS Learn) |
|---|---|---|
| **Vision & Strategy / Holistic Listening** | T−8 to T−6 wks | Align stakeholders, articulate business priorities, design the program with the *Holistic Listening Vision & Strategy Discovery Workbook* and the six **People Success Elements**. Set cadence deliberately (and watch survey/leader/HR **fatigue**). |
| **Survey design** | T−6 to T−3 wks | Build items on People Science design principles; pick program type (Recurring, Lifecycle, Ad-hoc, Always-On). |
| **Comms & launch** | T−6 wks → T0 | Intro/Vision emails ~6 wks out; launch comms; manager pre-brief. |
| **Fielding** | T0 → Tc (~2–3 wks) | Survey live; admins get live access, managers phased access. |
| **Results** | Tc + a few days | Managers receive results "within a few days"; **Nudges** fire to interpret + share. |
| **Manager conversations** | Tc + ≤2 wks | Manager shares results with the team within two weeks; runs an **ACT conversation** (Acknowledge → Collaborate → Take one step forward). |
| **Focus Areas & action** | Tc +1 to +8 wks | Pick **one** Focus Area; build an action plan; reminder emails at **week 4 and week 8**; Nudges sustain it. |
| **Sustain / re-listen** | mid-cycle | **Viva Pulse** follow-up on the chosen focus areas; **Team Effectiveness** re-survey 30–60 days after; Always-On for continuous input. |
| **Lifecycle (parallel, always-on)** | trigger-based | **Onboarding** at 30/60/90 days; **Exit** surveys on termination. |
| **Next cycle** | annual / semi-annual / quarterly | Microsoft explicitly recommends *shifting annual → semi-annual → quarterly* as maturity grows, fatigue permitting. |

**The load-bearing idea from Microsoft's People Science:** *"Reduce the time between feedback and action… create a simple check-in process… the key is not only to discuss action when the survey results come out."* The value isn't the survey — it's the cadence of conversations after it.

## 3. Eclectik's deepening overlay — what to do, and when

Map your offer onto every phase, not just design + readout. The phases you **already** touch are CS-config and the PS Insight Review. The phases below in **bold** are where you currently leave value (and relationship glue) on the table.

### The annual cadence calendar (relative to one survey close, Tc)

| When | Client-facing action (keep them informed) | Service to attach (deepen / grow) |
|---|---|---|
| **Tc − 10 to −8 wks** | Proactive "next-cycle planning" call | **Paid Vision & Strategy / Holistic Listening workshop** (you already did versions of this — Jazz "Vision & Strategy session", Maire scoping). Make it a named, priced engagement, not a freebie. |
| Tc − 6 wks | Survey design + comms kit | Item-strategy consulting; comms templates; manager pre-brief deck |
| Tc − 2 wks → Tc | Launch support, live response monitoring | CS "bucket of hours" for the live window (Syngenta model) |
| **Tc + 3–5 days** | "Results are live" note + exec preview | **Executive readout** as a distinct deliverable from the manager rollout |
| Tc + 1–2 wks | **Insight Review presentation** (your core PS deliverable) | Sell the *board narrative* version on top of the standard deck |
| **Tc + 2–4 wks** | **Manager enablement** | **ACT-conversation training + HRBP training** — you already do this for Serco and ETF; productize it as a standard SKU for every client. |
| **Tc + 4 & +8 wks** | **Action check-in** (mirrors MS week-4/week-8 reminders) | **Focus-Area facilitation / action-plan coaching retainer** — the single biggest missing recurring revenue line |
| **Tc + ~10 wks (mid-cycle)** | **Pulse follow-up** on the chosen focus areas | **Viva Pulse program design** + light analysis |
| **Tc + 3–6 months** | **Quarterly engagement-health check-in** | **Retainer / bucket-of-hours** for trend advisory + platform optimization (Syngenta explicitly wants "platform optimisation and new features") |
| Continuous | Lifecycle listening | **Onboarding 30/60/90 + Exit survey** setup & analysis; **Team Effectiveness** surveys for hot-spot teams; **Ad-hoc topic** surveys (AI adoption, change moments) |
| Tc + 9–10 months | Renewal + multi-cycle commitment | Annual program agreement; pre-purchased Insight Reviews (Sage already buys 6 up front — make that the default offer) |

### The cross-sell catalogue (services beyond technical updates)

**Already in your hands — productize these now:**
1. **Manager / HRBP enablement & ACT-conversation training** (done: Serco, ETF leader training). High margin, recurring, and it's the action half of the loop Microsoft says drives all the value.
2. **Vision & Strategy / Holistic Listening workshops** (done: Jazz, Maire scoping). Charge for the discovery instead of giving it away to win the survey.
3. **Executive / board-level narrative** from the PS analysis you already produce (the four-lens reads are wasted as a single slide).
4. **Linking engagement to business/objective signals** (Serco note: "linking their survey results to objective signals"). This is consulting gold and very sticky.

**Natural next step — Microsoft gives you the rails:**
5. **Viva Pulse mid-cycle follow-up** program (Microsoft's prescribed bridge between annual surveys).
6. **Lifecycle programs:** Onboarding 30/60/90 and Exit surveys — recurring, always-on, low-touch once configured.
7. **Team Effectiveness surveys** for specific teams (30–60 day re-survey cadence).
8. **Always-On / Ad-hoc topic surveys** tied to change moments (the AI-concern theme is screaming for this across Sage and others).

**Stickiness / retention plays:**
9. **Quarterly "engagement health" retainer** — converts the dead between-cycle months into booked, paid touchpoints and is your best defense against the GoDaddy→Qualtrics churn pattern.
10. **Platform optimization & feature-adoption sessions** (Syngenta wants this explicitly; bundle into the retainer).
11. **Data quality / integration support** (Maire's "bad data", NMHS Workday implementation) — unglamorous but it's where surveys die, and owning it makes you indispensable.

## 4. The three structural shifts (my recommendation)

1. **Sell the loop, not the survey.** Reframe the standard engagement from "survey + Insight Review" to a **12-month listening program** with the cadence in §3 baked in. Even if the dollar value per phase is small, the *booked touchpoints* are what stop churn.
2. **Monetize the action half.** Your PS analyses already prescribe next-best-actions; today you hand them over and walk away. The ACT-training + Focus-Area-facilitation + week-4/8 check-ins are a recurring SKU you can attach to *every* client immediately, with capability you've already proven (Serco, ETF).
3. **Make the between-cycle period a paid retainer, not silence.** A light quarterly check-in + Pulse + platform optimization converts your most vulnerable window (where GoDaddy decided to leave) into your stickiest revenue.

---

## 5. Guardrails (don't ignore these)

- **Fatigue is real and Microsoft flags it three ways** (survey, leader, HR). More listening is only sellable if it's matched with visible action — otherwise you accelerate the churn you're trying to prevent. Pulse and Always-On must be *light*.
- **Capacity is already tight.** The War room shows contractors stretched and hours running out mid-contract (Serco, Sage, Maire). Every new recurring SKU needs delivery capacity behind it — sell the retainer only where you can staff it. This is the real constraint, not demand.
- **Don't over-survey to justify the model.** The credible version of "more services" is *more action support and more advisory*, not necessarily more surveys.
