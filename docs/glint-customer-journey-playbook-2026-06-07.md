## 1. The clock you're working against

Microsoft's Glint lifecycle is the fixed timetable every client runs on, and it's more prescriptive than your delivery records treat it. Anchored to a survey cycle (**T0** = survey opens, **Tc** = survey closes):

| Phase | Lead | Timing | What happens (per MS Learn) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategy & listening design** | 🟢 PS | T−8 to −6 wks | Holistic Listening *Vision & Strategy Discovery Workbook*; align stakeholders to business priorities; the six **People Success Elements**; set cadence deliberately, watch survey/leader/HR **fatigue**. |
| **Configure & QA** | 🔵 CS | T−6 to −3 wks | Build items on People Science design principles; pick program type (Recurring, Lifecycle, Ad-hoc, Always-On); test; draft comms. |
| **Launch** | 🔵 CS | T−6 wks → T0 | Intro/Vision emails ~6 wks out; launch comms; manager pre-brief. |
| **Survey live** | 🔵 CS | T0 → Tc (~2–3 wks) | Monitor participation; **reminder to the full list 72 h before close**; default response window **14 days**. |
| **Close & results rollout** | 🟢 PS | Tc → Tc +7 days | Staged comms: *"survey closed / what's next"* to all employees **~1 day** after close; **results + next steps ~3–4 days** after; *"how to have effective conversations"* guide to managers & HRBPs **~5–7 days** after. |
| **Insights review & action planning** | 🟢 PS | Tc +1 to +3 wks | Insight Review presentation, **then the full action loop**: review → identify priorities → set goals → develop plans → communicate → implement → monitor → re-evaluate. Reminder emails at **week 4 and week 8**. |
| **Enablement & embedding** | 🟢 PS | Tc +2 wks → months | **Team Conversations** (managers get a personalised summary + run a guided team conversation) and **Nudges** (timely prompts to act) — the platform's built-in retention mechanism. HRBP/manager training; **Viva Pulse** mid-cycle; Team Effectiveness re-survey 30–60 days; Lifecycle (Onboarding 30/60/90, Exit) always-on. |
| **↻ Next cycle** | — | +6–12 months | Microsoft recommends *shifting annual → semi-annual → quarterly* as maturity grows, fatigue permitting. |

**The load-bearing point:** the official journey doesn't end at *"results delivered."* It ends — and restarts — at **action taken and measured.** *"Reduce the time between feedback and action… the key is not only to discuss action when the survey results come out."* That back half is the part you're largely **not** in.

## 2. What your delivery records actually show

Reading the `glint_delivery` rows, the work splits into two streams, weighted heavily to the technical one:

- **🔵 Customer Success (technical):** platform implementation, survey configuration, QA, launch, results release, admin training. Several engagements are *only* this — **SoFi, Douglas, ETF, Trane, PIMCO** are "CS Support" with `ps_hours = 0`. This is the commoditising middle.
- **🟢 People Science (advisory):** the Insight Review and final presentation (Paul / Avneeta / Kirsty). For most clients this is a **single deliverable** near `delivery_end`, then the project closes.

**The richer advisory touchpoints already exist — but as bespoke favours, not a product:**
Vision & Strategy session for **Warburtons** and **Almirall**'s 360; a Team Conversations session shaped for **Maire**; HRBP training + Executive Review for **Serco**; Office Hours for **Serco** and **ETF**; admin training for **ETF**; and the most interesting — **Serco** exploring *"linking survey results to objective signals."* These are the seeds of a full professional-services wrap.

**The tell — the action-planning gap.** **Dentsu**'s note: the client *"will do this internally"*; the engagement ends at the revised presentation. Per Microsoft's lifecycle that's the **highest-value phase**, and it's the one you most often hand back.

**The shape problem.** You're strong in the 🔵 technical middle and thin at both 🟢 ends — the strategic front and the embedding back. For a professional-services firm that's the wrong shape: the blue work is commoditising (**GoDaddy**'s note: *"moving to Qualtrics 2nd half of 2026"*), while the teal work is what's defensible and renewable.

Map your offer onto **every** phase, not just design + readout. Bold = where you currently leave value (and relationship glue) on the table.

## 4. The strategic punchline

Your customer journey, as built, **ends at `delivery_end`.** Nobody owns the months between one survey and the next. **Breitling** is the only client with a named *"monthly check-in"* rhythm — and notably it's framed around keeping the next contract warm. Everywhere else, the project closes and the relationship goes quiet.

That gap is almost certainly the engine behind the **dormancy** problem the BD dashboard flags (**11 of 33 customers** with no live or open project). Several dormant names — **Dentsu, M&C Saatchi, Boston Red Sox, Chugai, Pepkor** — are exactly clients whose `glint_delivery` shows a completed one-off insights engagement and then nothing. When the next survey comes around you're **re-pitching from cold instead of renewing from inside.** Against the €1M target you've modelled as at-risk, converting that cold re-pitch into a warm renewal is probably worth more than any new-logo effort.

**In one line:** stop selling discrete technical projects and start owning the **full annual listening loop** — especially the **action-planning back half** and the **between-cycle gap** — because that's both what Microsoft's lifecycle says drives outcomes and what turns your dormancy into recurring revenue.

### The three structural shifts
1. **Sell the loop, not the survey.** Reframe the standard engagement as a **12-month listening program** with the §3 cadence baked in. The *booked touchpoints* are what stop churn.
2. **Monetise the action half.** Your PS analyses already prescribe next-best-actions; today you hand them over and walk away. ACT-training + Focus-Area facilitation + week-4/8 check-ins are a recurring SKU you can attach to *every* client now, with capability you've proven (Serco, ETF).
3. **Make the between-cycle period a paid retainer, not silence.** A light quarterly check-in + Pulse + platform optimisation converts your most vulnerable window (where GoDaddy decided to leave) into your stickiest revenue.

---

## 5. Guardrails

- **Fatigue is real, and Microsoft flags it three ways** (survey, leader, HR). More listening only sells if matched with visible action — otherwise you accelerate the churn you're preventing. Pulse and Always-On must be *light*.
- **Capacity is already tight.** The War room shows contractors stretched and hours running out mid-contract (Serco, Sage, Maire). Every new recurring SKU needs delivery capacity — sell the retainer only where you can staff it. This is the real constraint, not demand.
- **Don't over-survey to justify the model.** The credible version of "more services" is *more action support and more advisory*, not necessarily more surveys.
- **Timings are Microsoft's official cadence, not necessarily each client's** — confirm against actual survey calendars. The CS/PS split is inferred from inconsistently-filled `service_type` + notes — treat as directional, not a precise audit.
